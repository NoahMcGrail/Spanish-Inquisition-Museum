What Was the Spanish Inquisition?

The Spanish Inquisition was a movement led by King Ferdinand and Queen Isabella in 1478 that lasted until 1834 in an attempt to maintain power over the people of Spain by censoring speech and religion. The main targets of the Inquisition were Jews, Muslims, and Protestants. However, there were also many other people targeted by the Inquisition. Anyone of these religions, or those who disagreed with clergy teachings were considered heretics and punished for their crimes. Sometimes when a suspected heretic denied their actions, they would be tortured, or even in extreme cases, killed. Many banned religious books were burned in Spain during this time along with news censorship. This all culminated in an age of fear and consequently scared away many intellectuals from Spain. Eventually the Inquisition died down due to the Age of Enlightenment ideals and loss of government power.
